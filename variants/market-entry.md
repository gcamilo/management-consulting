# Variant: Market Entry Analysis

**Mode:** Full Case (or Client Deliverable if a memo/deck is requested).

## When to use

The user asks about entering a new market -- new geography, new segment, new product category, or new channel.

## Context to establish (assume if missing, flag as `[A]`)

If the user hasn't specified, state assumptions upfront and proceed. Do NOT stall waiting for answers.

1. **Which market?** Geography, segment, or vertical -- be precise.
2. **Why now?** What triggered this question? (Competitor moved, customer request, board mandate, opportunity spotted.)
3. **What's the investment appetite?** Rough budget range and timeline to breakeven.
4. **What's the fallback?** If we don't enter, what happens? (This defines the counterfactual.)
5. **Who decides?** CEO, board, business unit head -- and what's their decision criteria?

If 3+ are missing, name plausible defaults (e.g., "[A] Assuming $5–10M investment envelope, 24-month payback target, CEO+board approval") and continue.

## Required frameworks

### 1. Five Forces (industry attractiveness)

Map the target market's structural dynamics. Focus on:
- **Buyer power** -- concentrated or fragmented customers?
- **Barriers to entry** -- what stops us, and what stops the next entrant after us?
- **Rivalry intensity** -- is this a growing pie or a knife fight for share?

Do not produce a generic Five Forces. Populate every force with specific data about THIS market.

### 2. TAM / SAM / SOM (market sizing)

- **TAM:** Total addressable market for the product category (top-down from industry reports + bottom-up from unit economics).
- **SAM:** Serviceable addressable market given our current capabilities, geography, and go-to-market.
- **SOM:** Realistic share we can capture in 24 months, based on comparable entry case studies or analogues.

Never fabricate TAM numbers. If data is unavailable, describe exactly what data you need and from where (e.g., "Need Gartner/IDC report on X" or "Need to survey Y customers").

### 3. Competitive positioning

- Map top 3-5 competitors on the dimensions that matter to the ICP.
- Identify whitespace: where is no one competing well?
- Assess differentiation durability: how long before incumbents close the gap?

## Required deliverables

1. **Market sizing summary** -- TAM/SAM/SOM with sources and assumptions labeled.
2. **Competitive landscape diagram** -- 2x2 or positioning map with named competitors.
3. **Entry strategy recommendation** -- mode of entry (organic, partnership, acquisition), timeline, investment required, key milestones.
4. **Kill tests** -- 2-3 conditions that, if false, mean "don't enter."

## Issue tree structure

```
Should [Company] enter [Market] by [Date]?
├── 1. Is the market attractive enough?
│   ├── TAM/SAM/SOM
│   ├── Growth trajectory
│   └── Structural profitability (Five Forces)
├── 2. Can we win?
│   ├── Competitive gaps we can exploit
│   ├── Differentiation durability
│   └── Customer willingness to switch
├── 3. Is the investment justified?
│   ├── Required capital and time-to-breakeven
│   ├── Opportunity cost of alternatives
│   └── Funding/runway constraints
└── 4. Can we execute?
    ├── Capabilities gap (team, tech, regulatory)
    ├── Partnership or acquisition options
    └── Timeline feasibility
```

## Common mistakes in market entry

- Assuming TAM = opportunity (SAM is what matters).
- Ignoring switching costs from the customer's perspective.
- Underestimating the time to build local credibility in a new geography.
- Comparing entry cost to zero instead of to the next-best alternative use of capital.
