# Variant: Organizational Effectiveness & Change Management

**Mode:** Full Case (or Client Deliverable if a roadmap deck is requested).

Use when the problem is about organizational performance, culture, structure, talent, or implementing change.

## Context to establish (assume if missing, flag as `[A]`)

If the user hasn't specified, state assumptions upfront and proceed. Do NOT stall waiting for answers.

1. What specific organizational outcome are you trying to improve? (speed, quality, cost, retention, innovation)
2. Is this a diagnosis ("why aren't we performing?") or a transformation ("how do we change?")?
3. What's been tried before? Why didn't it work?
4. Who are the key stakeholders, and who resists change?
5. What's the timeline and appetite for disruption?

## Required frameworks

- **7S** — diagnose alignment across strategy, structure, systems, shared values, skills, staff, style
- **Influence Model** — identify levers for behavior change: role modeling, understanding, confidence, reinforcement
- **ADKAR** (for individual change) — Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, Reinforcement
- **Kotter's 8-Step** (for organizational change) — urgency, coalition, vision, communicate, empower, wins, build, anchor

## Issue tree structure

```
Why is the organization underperforming?
├── Strategy alignment
│   ├── Strategy is clear but not communicated
│   └── Strategy has changed but structure hasn't
├── Structural barriers
│   ├── Decision rights unclear (use RAPID)
│   ├── Silos prevent cross-functional work
│   └── Spans of control too wide/narrow
├── Talent & capability
│   ├── Key roles unfilled or mismatched
│   ├── Skills gap for new strategy
│   └── Retention of top performers
├── Culture & leadership
│   ├── Leaders don't model desired behavior
│   ├── Incentives reward old behavior
│   └── No psychological safety for risk-taking
└── Systems & processes
    ├── Metrics don't align with goals
    ├── Technology constrains new ways of working
    └── Approval processes too slow
```

## Required deliverables

1. **7S diagnostic** — current state assessment across all 7 elements, with gaps highlighted
2. **Stakeholder map** — power/interest grid with key players positioned
3. **Change roadmap** — phased plan: quick wins (30 days), structural changes (90 days), culture shifts (6-12 months)
4. **Risk matrix** — change risks (resistance, fatigue, capability gaps) with mitigations

## Common mistakes

- Treating org problems as strategy problems (restructuring won't fix a bad strategy)
- Ignoring the informal organization (who actually has influence vs who has the title)
- Underestimating change fatigue (if 3 reorgs happened in 2 years, another one isn't the answer)
- Designing the "perfect" org chart without considering who fills the boxes
- Assuming communication = understanding (saying it once is not change management)
