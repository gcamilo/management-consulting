# Recommendation: Good vs Bad

## Scenario

A B2B SaaS company has declining Net Revenue Retention (NRR) -- dropped from 115% to 102% over 3 quarters.

---

## BAD recommendation

> "The company should improve customer experience to reduce churn. We recommend investing in customer success, improving the product, and building stronger relationships with key accounts. This will help retain customers and grow revenue."

**What's wrong:**
- **Not specific.** "Improve customer experience" is a platitude, not a recommendation.
- **No owner.** Who does what? The CEO? The VP of CS? The PM?
- **No timeline.** "Invest in customer success" -- starting when? Measured by when?
- **No evidence.** Why customer experience? What data points to this as the root cause?
- **No risks.** What happens if this doesn't work? What are the alternatives?
- **No "so what."** What's the financial impact of NRR dropping to 95%? To 110%?

---

## GOOD recommendation

**BOTTOM LINE:** Halt the NRR decline by Q3 2026 through a targeted save program for the 23 accounts in the "high-risk" cohort, which represent 68% of projected churn revenue.

**SO WHAT:**
1. **Root cause is onboarding failure, not product gaps** [F] -- Exit survey data (n=41 churned accounts, 2025) shows 71% cite "never reached first value milestone." Only 12% cite missing features. Product investment alone won't fix this.
2. **The damage is concentrated** [F] -- 23 of 340 accounts (7%) represent $1.8M ARR at risk. These share a profile: joined in Q2-Q3 2025, <40% feature adoption at day 90, no executive sponsor identified. A focused intervention on these 23 is higher-ROI than a broad CS overhaul.
3. **A 90-day save playbook has precedent internally** [I] -- The EMEA team ran a similar intervention in Q4 2025 and recovered 8 of 11 at-risk accounts ($620K ARR saved, $45K program cost). US team can replicate with adaptation. Confidence: 70% that recovery rate holds.

**KEY RISKS:**
1. **Execution risk (P=30%):** The US CS team is at 60% headcount vs plan. Running the save program with current staff may mean deprioritizing new customer onboarding, creating a future churn cohort. *Mitigation:* Hire 2 contract CSMs for 6 months ($90K total) to cover new accounts.
2. **Root cause misdiagnosis (P=20%):** If the real driver is competitive displacement (not onboarding), the save program won't work. *Mitigation:* Instrument the first 5 saves with detailed loss-reason tracking. If <3 of 5 respond to onboarding intervention by week 6, pivot to competitive analysis.

**COUNTER-ARGUMENT:** A smart opponent would say: "You're treating symptoms. If the product can't deliver value without hand-holding, a save program just delays churn. Invest in product-led onboarding instead." This is valid long-term -- and the company should pursue PLG onboarding in parallel (H2 2026 roadmap item). But the 23 at-risk accounts will churn in <90 days. PLG onboarding takes 6+ months to build and measure. The save program buys time. Both are needed; sequence matters.
