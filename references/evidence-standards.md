# Evidence Standards

Every claim in a consulting deliverable must be labeled and sourced.

## Claim types

| Type | Symbol | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Fact** | [F] | Observed, sourced, verifiable | "Revenue was $142M in FY24 (10-K p.34)" |
| **Inference** | [I] | Derived from facts via logic | "Revenue growth is decelerating (24% → 18% → 12%)" |
| **Assumption** | [A] | Gap filled by judgment | "We assume 15% market growth (in line with analyst consensus)" |
| **Estimate** | [E] | Calculated from assumptions + data | "TAM = 500K users x $200 ARPU = $100M" |

## Rules

1. Never present assumptions as facts
2. Every number needs: **value + unit + time period + source**
   - Bad: "Revenue is $142M"
   - Good: "Revenue was $142M in FY2024 (Annual Report, p.34)"
3. State confidence for assumptions: "We assume 15% growth (70% confidence — could be 10-20%)"
4. Cite what would change the answer: "If growth is <10%, the NPV turns negative"
5. Prefer base rates over narratives: "3 of 5 similar market entries failed" > "this feels risky"
6. Triangulate: at least 2 independent sources for key claims
7. Flag single-source dependencies explicitly
